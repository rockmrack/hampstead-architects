## The Challenge

Creating basement space beneath Victorian terraces is one of London's most complex construction challenges. This Primrose Hill property presented multiple technical hurdles:

- **Shallow Foundations**: Original 1880s brick strip footings only 600mm deep
- **Party Walls**: Shared walls on both sides requiring neighbor consent and protection
- **High Water Table**: Groundwater at 2.5m depth requiring permanent drainage
- **Access**: Narrow side return limiting plant and material movement
- **Neighbors**: Managing three Party Wall Awards with vigilant adjoining owners

## Our Approach

### Phase 1: Planning & Party Wall Agreements

Before any work began, we secured:
- Detailed structural survey by specialist basement engineer
- Party Wall Awards with neighbors on both sides and to the rear
- Pre-construction condition surveys on all three adjoining properties
- Camden Planning approval with Construction Management Plan
- Building Control approval for structural calculations

### Phase 2: Temporary Works & Underpinning

The excavation sequence was critical:
- **Mini-piled Wall**: 15no. CFA piles at 1.2m centers to form temporary retaining wall
- **Sequential Underpinning**: Party walls underpinned in 1m stages, never more than 2 bays at once
- **Load Transfer**: Existing walls supported on new RC piled foundations
- **Dewatering**: Wellpoint system to lower water table during construction
- **Monitoring**: Daily readings on crack monitors and precise level surveys

### Phase 3: Basement Construction

Once underpinning was complete:
- **RC Slab**: 300mm thick reinforced concrete base slab with A393 mesh
- **RC Walls**: 250mm thick reinforced concrete perimeter walls
- **Waterproofing**: Type A barrier protection using bitumen membrane
- **Cavity Drain**: Type C system as backup with perimeter drainage channel
- **Sump Pump**: Automatic pumped drainage system for long-term water management
- **Structural Slab**: New RC ground floor slab replacing original suspended timber

### Phase 4: Lightwell & Internal Fit-Out

To prevent a dark, oppressive space:
- **Glass Lightwell**: 3m × 2m rear lightwell with structural glass floor at ground level
- **Glazed Doors**: Full-height sliding glass doors connecting basement to rear courtyard
- **Ceiling Height**: 2.7m clear height achieved through deep excavation
- **Finishes**: Micro-cement polished floors, recessed lighting, acoustic ceiling treatments
- **MVHR**: Mechanical ventilation with heat recovery for air quality

## The Result

The basement now provides 85 sqm of high-quality living space. The home cinema features acoustic treatments and bespoke joinery. The gym has rubber flooring and mirrored walls. The playroom connects directly to the rear garden via the lightwell.

**No structural movement** was detected in any neighboring property. All three party wall awards were settled without dispute.

## Technical Specifications

- **Excavation Depth**: 1.8m below existing floor level
- **Foundation Type**: Mini-piled underpinning (450mm diameter CFA piles)
- **Waterproofing**: Type A bitumen membrane + Type C cavity drainage
- **Drainage**: Pumped sump system with backup battery
- **Ventilation**: MVHR system (200 m³/h capacity)
- **Acoustic Performance**: 60dB sound reduction (home cinema)
- **Construction Duration**: 16 weeks
- **Party Wall Process**: 8 months (including consents and surveys)

## Challenges Overcome

1. **Groundwater Ingress**: Managed through wellpoint dewatering during construction and permanent sump system
2. **Party Wall Settlement**: Zero measurable movement through careful underpinning sequence
3. **Access Constraints**: All spoil removed through house (20 tonnes via wheelbarrow relay)
4. **Water Table**: Permanent drainage system tested to 1-in-100-year flood scenario
5. **Neighbor Relations**: Weekly progress reports and open-door policy maintained positive relationships
